The read

The cleared model is the easy part. The orchestration around it is not.

The UW Medical Center study on valvular disease and atrial fibrillation is doing real work for the clinical story. So is Dr. McCabe on the record. So are the 40,000 users who have already made the Stethophone corpus bigger than most audio ML shops will ever build.

Two questions sit underneath that. First, which parts of what Sparrow ships are genuinely AI problems, and which are deterministic code dressed in an AI wrapper. Second, which parts of the product organisation are re-deciding the consumer-and-clinician split every sprint because nobody has drawn the architectural line once.

Neither question is about the bioacoustic model. Both are about what sits around it.

Who this is for. the Head of AI and Data Science, the CPO, with executive sponsorship.

What it is. A thirty-minute conversation about the decision layer above the regulated model. Scoped, written, auditable.

Out of scope. Anything that touches the cleared model, the PHI plane, or your FDA change-control surface.

The 60 / 30 / 10 read

Applied to Sparrow.

Most organisations automate the wrong things. The discipline is deciding which layer each problem belongs on. In most stacks, 60 percent of the work is traditional code and database, 30 percent is rule-based logic, 10 percent is actually AI. Sparrow is the unusual case where the cleared bioacoustic model is genuinely the AI tier and should stay there, protected from scope creep, with strict change-control discipline.

60%Code & data

The plumbing.

Handoff between a consumer flag and a clinician inbox. Recording provenance. EHR-friendly outputs. Not AI. Traditional software, done carefully.

30%Rule-based

The policy layer.

When does a retrain live inside the existing clearance. When does it trigger a fresh submission. Who signs. This is where most clinical-AI companies lose months.

10%Real AI

The cleared model.

The bioacoustic pipeline Sparrow already owns. This tier should stay narrow, defended, and boring in the best sense. Out of scope for any engagement.

The useful work sits in the 60 and the 30. Who routes a consumer flag into a clinician workflow. How a model-update decision moves from the AI team through regulatory review to release. Where the line is drawn between consumer telemetry and PRO-grade clinical data. All of it is the orchestration layer around the AI, and it is where a short written framework beats a long engineering sprint.

The case

Feeld: product company, CTO buyer, same shape of problem.

Feeld engaged Eduba for a scoped sprint covering workshop, advisory calls, Organisational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework. The deliverable was a written framework for which decisions live where, who makes them, and how they get audited.

The same sprint applied to Sparrow would start with the consumer-PRO split and end with a decision map the clinical, regulatory, and product leads all share.
